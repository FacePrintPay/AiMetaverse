THE PLATFORM

MyBuyo™ Biometric Gateway

MyBuyo™ is a retail biometric payment gateway built by FacePrintPay Inc. — a sovereign AI and identity infrastructure company headquartered in North Carolina. The platform is designed specifically for self-checkout and integrated POS environments.

👁

Face Recognition Pay

Customers enroll once, pay by looking at the terminal. No phone. No card. No friction. Sub-second authentication tied to encrypted biometric templates — never raw biometric data stored.

🖐

Fingerprint + Palm

Multi-modal biometric options give customers choice. Fingerprint on kiosk rail or palm hover — both tied to the same secure vault. Supports customers who opt out of facial recognition.

🔗

Self-Checkout Integration

Drop-in SDK and API layer designed for existing self-checkout terminals. Compatible with major grocery checkout hardware. Minimal retrofit required — biometrics replace PIN pad step.

🏛

TotalRecall™ Vault

SHA256-anchored forensic logging of every transaction event. Immutable audit trail with timestamp and terminal ID. Built-in compliance infrastructure for Ahold Delhaize enterprise security standards.

🛡

Privacy-First Architecture

Zero raw biometric storage. All templates are encrypted mathematical representations. Consent-based enrollment workflow. Built with BIPA, state biometric privacy law, and PCI DSS alignment in mind.

📊

Merchant Analytics Dashboard

Real-time throughput data, transaction velocity, enrolled customer metrics, and shrink-reduction analytics. Gives store management and corporate visibility into pilot performance at every level.

PILOT STRUCTURE

The 3-Phase Rollout Plan

We propose a structured, low-risk pilot beginning with a single North Carolina store — ideally one with existing self-checkout infrastructure — with clear success metrics and an expansion pathway tied to quantifiable outcomes.

PHASE 01
Single-Store Pilot — Proof of Concept
Deploy MyBuyo™ on 2–4 self-checkout kiosks at one designated Food Lion location. Customer enrollment station at store entrance. Full biometric payment capability alongside existing payment methods — customers opt in voluntarily. Forensic logging active from Day 1 via TotalRecall™.
📅 MONTHS 1–3 · KPIs: Enrollment rate, transaction velocity, customer satisfaction NPS, error rate
PHASE 02
Regional Expansion — 5–10 Stores
Based on Phase 1 data, expand to 5–10 NC/SC locations. Introduce MyBuyo™ loyalty integration — biometric identity linked to Food Lion MVP loyalty program for seamless rewards. Add Food Lion-branded enrollment portal (web + in-app). Deeper analytics dashboard deployed to regional management.
📅 MONTHS 4–9 · KPIs: Checkout time reduction %, repeat biometric usage rate, shrink delta
PHASE 03
Chain-Wide Rollout + Acquisition / Licensing Discussion
Full deployment across Food Lion's 1,100+ store network. Present performance data to Ahold Delhaize USA executive team. Open formal discussions on technology licensing, acquisition, or exclusive partnership structure. MyBuyo™ becomes a Food Lion proprietary capability — a competitive moat no Southeast competitor can replicate.
📅 MONTHS 10–18 · MILESTONE: Ahold Delhaize boardroom presentation

SUBJECT: Letter of Intent — MyBuyo™ Biometric Payment Gateway Pilot Program

Dear Food Lion Leadership Team,

This Letter of Intent ("LOI") is submitted by FacePrintPay Inc., a technology company specializing in sovereign biometric identity infrastructure and retail payment systems, to formally express our intent to partner with Food Lion, LLC ("Food Lion") on a mutually beneficial pilot program deploying the MyBuyo™ Retail Biometric Payment Gateway at one or more Food Lion retail locations within the state of North Carolina.

1. PROPOSED PARTNERSHIP

FacePrintPay Inc. proposes to deploy its proprietary MyBuyo™ biometric payment system — supporting facial recognition, fingerprint, and palm-print payment authentication — integrated with Food Lion's existing self-checkout terminal infrastructure. The pilot would allow Food Lion customers to voluntarily enroll in biometric payment, completing transactions without physical cards, mobile devices, or PIN entry.

2. PILOT SCOPE

Phase 1 of the proposed pilot would be conducted at a single Food Lion store location (to be mutually agreed upon) over a period of approximately ninety (90) days, encompassing 2–4 self-checkout lanes. FacePrintPay Inc. will provide all integration engineering, system installation support, staff training materials, and a real-time analytics dashboard at no additional cost to Food Lion during the pilot period.

3. EXCLUSIVITY CONSIDERATION

In exchange for Food Lion's participation as a pilot partner, FacePrintPay Inc. is prepared to offer Food Lion a right of first negotiation on an exclusive Southeast U.S. grocery partnership for a period of twelve (12) months from the conclusion of Phase 1, subject to formal agreement terms.

4. PRIVACY & COMPLIANCE

All biometric data collected through the MyBuyo™ system is processed in accordance with applicable state biometric privacy laws, PCI DSS standards, and Ahold Delhaize enterprise data governance requirements. No raw biometric data is stored; all templates are encrypted representations. Customer enrollment is strictly opt-in with written consent obtained at enrollment.

5. NON-BINDING NATURE

This Letter of Intent is non-binding and does not constitute a legally enforceable agreement. It is intended solely to express the mutual interest of both parties in exploring the proposed partnership and to guide subsequent good-faith negotiations toward a formal Pilot Agreement.

6. NEXT STEPS

FacePrintPay Inc. respectfully requests an introductory meeting with Food Lion's technology and operations leadership to present a full live demonstration of the MyBuyo™ platform and discuss pilot implementation logistics. We are prepared to travel to Salisbury, NC at Food Lion's convenience.
